
## On the Vortices-Within-Vortices Dataset

Bürger et al. visualized vortices within vortices [[vortices]](#vortices) (I cited this work in [[complements]](#complements)).
Their dataset seems to be the Forced Isotropic Turbulence Data Set [[isotropicpaper]](#isotropicpaper) from Johns Hopkins Turbulence Database, available for download [[isotropic]](#isotropic). Some more description, on the database and also the simulation and the dataset, are given by Yi et al. [[database]](#database).

The simulation by Wan et al. [[isotropicpaper]](#isotropicpaper) is cited by publications on fluid feature identification [[identification]](#identification), AI use [[ai2021]](#ai2021) [[ai2024]](#ai2024) [[ai2025]](#ai2025) among others [[topology]](#topology). It is noteworthy that this work by Wan et al. from 2012 has suddenly started receiving citations in the 2020s as the latest AI wave [[ai2021]](#ai2021) took off, especially since 2024, right after the transformer model significantly increased the AI investment.
